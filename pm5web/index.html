Rule-based observations after every workout — no AI required. Plain English, evidence-backed, never dense:

Best drive length in 3 weeks.

⚠ WARNING
Late-piece fade detected.
Your average watts dropped 8.4% in the final quarter.
🛠 TECHNIQUE
Peak force moved later.

❤ FITNESS
Lower heart rate for same pace.
Compared with similar sessions, your HR was 6 bpm lower at the same split.
🎯 RECOMMENDATION
Recommended next focus.
Steady-state session at 18–20 spm while keeping drive length above 1.48 m.

Rowing mechanics across sessions.
A. Force-curve comparison
Toggle between Average / Best / First 25% / Last 25% / Selected. See peak force, peak timing, drive length, slip (catch + release), drive ratio side-by-side.
B. Technique trend cards
Drive length over time (1.51 m, ↑ 3.2% vs recent average). Peak timing trend (41%, stable). Slip trend (15°, ↓ improved by 2°).
C. Stroke consistency
Coefficient-of-variation score per metric: split, watts, drive length, peak timing, and an overall consistency rollup.
D. Technique diagnosis
Rule-based root-cause labels: "Shortening drive — length dropped 6.1%, watts dropped 7.8%, rate stable." Identifies whether the issue is power, length, timing, or consistency.

Endurance, heart rate, and load.
A. Heart-rate zones
Time in zone (Z1–Z5) bar chart. Existing zone tracking from v1.0 feeds in directly.
B. Training load
Today / last 7 days / last 30 days TRIMP rollup. Banister-style, already computed live; just needs persistence + aggregation.
C. HR drift
First-half watts/HR vs second-half watts/HR ratio. Surfaces aerobic drift even when split looks identical.
D. Similar-session fitness trend
Only compares like-to-like: 30-min steady state vs other 30-min steady states; 2k tests vs other 2k tests. Never compares sprint to steady-state.

Track what you're working toward.
Goal types
Distance PR · Time PR · Weekly metres · Monthly metres · Training consistency · Technique goal.
Example goals
Sub-7 2k — current 7:08.4, target 6:59.9, progress 73%.
Hold 1.50 m drive length — current 1.46 m, target 1.50 m, progress 62%.
Weekly metres — current 42,850 m, target 60,000 m, progress 71%.
Progress calculation
Time goals: (start − current) / (start − target).
Higher-is-better: (current − start) / (target − start).
Clamped 0–1.
Why this matters
Athletes see progress before hitting the final goal — partial wins keep training sticky.
